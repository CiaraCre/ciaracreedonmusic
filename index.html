About Ciara

A voice to remember

Hi, I'm Ciara — an experienced singer based in Cork, Ireland. I have a BA in Music from UCC and I am a qualified music teacher. I play guitar and piano, and I have performed in many churches, hotels and venues across Munster.

I have a passion for bringing music to life on special days. I'll work with you to craft the perfect soundtrack for your ceremony — whether that's a joyful wedding or a tender farewell.

I perform across all genres and take pride in learning songs that are personally meaningful to each couple or family. Every performance is tailored, rehearsed, and delivered with warmth and professionalism.

“Music should feel personal, graceful, and completely in tune with the moment.”

Church Wedding Ceremony

A guide to your music

Planning your church ceremony music can feel overwhelming, so here's a simple breakdown of each moment and what works best.

1

Processional

This is your entrance — music begins as soon as the first person in the bridal party walks through the doors and continues until the priest greets you and you're seated. Choose something that feels personal and sets the tone for your day.

2

Lighting of the Individual Candles

The bride and groom each light a candle representing themselves. This moment is brief — usually around 20–30 seconds — so an instrumental piece works beautifully here.

3

Responsorial Psalm

A reflective moment during the liturgy of the word.

4

Gospel Acclamation

A short, joyful response before the Gospel reading.

5

Lighting of the Marriage Candle

Together, the couple light a third candle symbolising their union. Like the individual candles, this takes around 20–30 seconds, so an instrumental piece is ideal — the same song used earlier works perfectly here too.

6

Offertory

Music to accompany the presentation of the gifts.

7

Sign of Peace

A gentle, peaceful moment in the ceremony. A short instrumental or verse of a song will suffice.

8

Communion

Liturgical music is best here. Depending on the size of your congregation, you'll likely need one or two hymns — I'd always recommend having two prepared just in case.

9

Signing of the Civil Register

This is where you sign the legal marriage documents, and as the formal mass is over, there's a little more freedom with song choice.

10

Recessional

Time to celebrate — you're married! The recessional is the bridal party's exit.
